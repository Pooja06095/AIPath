➕ What are Operators?

Operators are symbols that perform different operations on values and variables. They help us perform calculations, comparisons and logical operations.

💡 Real Life Example

Think of operators like mathematical symbols. Just as + adds two numbers and - subtracts them, JavaScript operators perform different tasks on data.

➕ Arithmetic Operators

Operator Description Example
+ Addition 10 + 5 = 15
- Subtraction 10 - 5 = 5
* Multiplication 10 * 5 = 50
/ Division 10 / 5 = 2
% Modulus 10 % 3 = 1

⚖️ Comparison Operators

  • == → Equal to
  • === → Strict Equal
  • != → Not Equal
  • > → Greater Than
  • << /strong> → Less Than
  • >= → Greater Than or Equal
  • <=< /strong> → Less Than or Equal

💻 Example


        let a = 10;
        let b = 5;
        
        console.log("Addition:", a + b);
        console.log("Subtraction:", a - b);
        console.log("Multiplication:", a * b);
        console.log("Division:", a / b);
        console.log("Modulus:", a % b);

🖥 Output

        Addition: 15
        Subtraction: 5
        Multiplication: 50
        Division: 2
        Modulus: 0

🎯 Common Interview Question

Q. What is the difference between == and === in JavaScript?

Answer: == compares only values, while === compares both value and data type.

🧠 Did You Know?

The === operator is recommended over == because it prevents unexpected type conversion and makes your code safer.

💡 Key Points

  • Operators perform operations on values and variables.
  • Arithmetic operators are used for calculations.
  • Comparison operators compare two values.
  • Logical operators combine conditions.
  • Prefer === over ==.
